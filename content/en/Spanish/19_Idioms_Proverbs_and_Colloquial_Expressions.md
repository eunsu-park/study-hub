## 1. Body-Related Idioms

| Idiom | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Costar un ojo de la cara | To cost an eye from the face | To be very expensive |
| Estar hasta las narices | To be up to the nose | To be fed up |
| No tener pelos en la lengua | To have no hairs on the tongue | To speak bluntly |
| Meter la pata | To put in the foot/paw | To make a blunder |
| Tomar el pelo a alguien | To pull someone's hair | To tease, to kid |
| Dar en el clavo | To hit the nail | To hit the nail on the head |
| Estar con el agua al cuello | To have water at the neck | To be in deep trouble |
| Hacer la vista gorda | To make the sight fat | To turn a blind eye |
| Quedarse con la boca abierta | To be left with mouth open | To be amazed |
| Ser uña y carne | To be nail and flesh | To be inseparable |

### Example Sentences

- Ese restaurante **cuesta un ojo de la cara**. (That restaurant is very expensive.)
- ¡**Metí la pata** en la reunión! (I made a blunder at the meeting!)
- No me **tomes el pelo**, dime la verdad. (Don't kid me, tell me the truth.)
- María y yo somos **uña y carne**. (María and I are inseparable.)

## 3. Food-Related Idioms

| Idiom | Literal Meaning | Actual Meaning |
|-------|----------------|---------------|
| Pan comido | Eaten bread | A piece of cake (easy) |
| Importar un pepino | To matter a cucumber | To not care at all |
| Ponerse como un tomate | To turn like a tomato | To blush, turn red |
| Dar calabazas | To give pumpkins | To reject (romantically) |
| Ser la media naranja | To be the half orange | To be one's soulmate |
| Ir al grano | To go to the grain | To get to the point |
| Estar en su salsa | To be in one's sauce | To be in one's element |
| Tener mala leche | To have bad milk | To have a bad temper |
| Comerse el coco | To eat one's coconut | To overthink |
| Ponerse las pilas | To put in the batteries | To get one's act together |

### Example Sentences

- Este examen es **pan comido**. (This exam is a piece of cake.)
- Me importa un **pepino** lo que digan. (I couldn't care less what they say.)
- Se puso como un **tomate** cuando le hablé. (He/she turned red when I spoke to them.)
- ¡Deja de **comerte el coco** y actúa! (Stop overthinking and act!)

## 10. Expressing Emotions Colloquially

### Joy and Excitement

| Expression | Context |
|-----------|---------|
| ¡Qué alegría! | What joy! |
| ¡Genial! / ¡Estupendo! / ¡Fenomenal! | Awesome! / Terrific! / Fantastic! |
| ¡Qué ilusión! | I'm so excited! (Spain) |
| ¡No me lo puedo creer! | I can't believe it! |
| ¡Me hace muy feliz! | It makes me so happy! |

### Sadness and Disappointment

| Expression | Context |
|-----------|---------|
| ¡Qué pena! | What a shame! |
| ¡Qué decepción! | What a disappointment! |
| Me siento fatal. | I feel terrible. |
| No puedo más. | I can't take it anymore. |

### Anger and Annoyance

| Expression | Context |
|-----------|---------|
| ¡Qué rabia! | How annoying! |
| ¡Es el colmo! | That's the last straw! |
| ¡Ya está bien! | That's enough! |
| ¡Basta! | Enough! |
| ¡No aguanto más! | I can't stand it anymore! |
| Me tiene harto/a. | I'm fed up with it/them. |

|-----------|---------|

## 11. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: Match the Idiom

Match each idiom with its meaning.

1. Costar un ojo de la cara        a) To make a mistake
2. Meter la pata                   b) To be very expensive
3. Pan comido                      c) To get to the point
4. Ir al grano                     d) A piece of cake
5. Quedarse en blanco              e) To draw a blank

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1-b, 2-a, 3-d, 4-c, 5-e

</details>

### Exercise 2: Complete with Body Parts

Fill in the missing body part.

1. Costar un ojo de la ________ (to be very expensive)
2. Estar hasta las ________ (to be fed up)
3. No tener pelos en la ________ (to speak bluntly)
4. Quedarse con la ________ abierta (to be amazed)
5. Ser ________ y carne (to be inseparable)

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **cara** (face) 2. **narices** (nose) 3. **lengua** (tongue)
4. **boca** (mouth) 5. **uña** (nail)

</details>

### Exercise 3: Proverb Completion

Complete the proverbs from memory.

1. Más vale tarde que ________
2. No es oro todo lo que ________
3. Dime con quién andas y te diré quién ________
4. A quien madruga, Dios le ________
5. La práctica hace al ________

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **nunca** (Better late than never)
2. **reluce** (All that glitters is not gold)
3. **eres** (Tell me who your friends are...)
4. **ayuda** (The early bird catches the worm)
5. **maestro** (Practice makes perfect)

</details>

### Exercise 4: Context Match

Which expression would you use in each situation?

1. Your friend got a terrible haircut but asks if you like it.
2. You passed a very difficult exam easily.
3. You're trying to explain something and someone keeps going off topic.
4. You're exhausted after a 12-hour workday.
5. A friend and you are inseparable.

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Hacer la vista gorda** (Turn a blind eye) or be diplomatic
2. ¡Fue **pan comido**! (It was a piece of cake!)
3. ¡Ve **al grano**! (Get to the point!)
4. Estoy **hecho/a polvo**. (I'm exhausted.)
5. Somos **uña y carne**. (We're inseparable.)

</details>
