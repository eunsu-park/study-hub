## 1. Reading Strategies Overview

```
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|            READING STRATEGIES FOR SPANISH                 |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                          |
|  BEFORE reading:                                         |
|  1. Look at the title, headings, images                  |
|  2. Predict what the text is about                       |
|  3. Activate background knowledge                        |
|                                                          |
|  DURING reading:                                         |
|  4. Don't stop at every unknown word                     |
|  5. Use context clues to guess meaning                   |
|  6. Look for cognates (words similar to English)         |
|  7. Pay attention to connecting words                    |
|                                                          |
|  AFTER reading:                                          |
|  8. Summarize the main idea                              |
|  9. Review unknown vocabulary                            |
|  10. Re-read difficult sections                          |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
```

> **Key principle**: You do NOT need to understand every word to understand a text.
> Focus on the main ideas first, then fill in details.

## 2. Skimming and Scanning

### Skimming (Lectura rápida)

Purpose: Get the **general idea** of a text quickly.

How to skim:
- Read the title and subtitles
- Read the first sentence of each paragraph
- Look at any bold or italicized words
- Read the conclusion

### Scanning (Lectura selectiva)

Purpose: Find **specific information** quickly.

How to scan:
- Know what you're looking for before you start
- Move your eyes quickly over the text
- Look for key words, numbers, names, dates
- Stop only when you find the target information

### Intensive Reading (Lectura intensiva)

Purpose: Understand a text **in detail**.

When to use:
- Study materials, instructions, contracts
- After skimming to identify important sections
- When preparing for exams

## 3. Context Clues

When you encounter an unknown word, look for these clues:

### Type 1: Definition Clues

The text defines the word directly:
> "La **biodiversidad**, es decir, la variedad de especies animales y vegetales..."
> (Biodiversity, that is, the variety of animal and plant species...)

Signal phrases: **es decir** (that is), **o sea** (in other words), **esto es** (this is)

### Type 2: Synonym Clues

A synonym appears nearby:
> "Estaba **exhausto**, completamente **agotado** después del viaje."
> (He was exhausted, completely worn out after the trip.)

### Type 3: Antonym Clues

An opposite word provides contrast:
> "No era **tímido** sino muy **extrovertido**."
> (He was not shy but very extroverted.)

Signal words: **sino** (but rather), **en cambio** (on the other hand), **a diferencia de** (unlike)

### Type 4: Example Clues

Examples clarify the meaning:
> "Los **cítricos**, como las naranjas, los limones y las mandarinas..."
> (Citrus fruits, like oranges, lemons, and tangerines...)

Signal phrases: **como** (like), **por ejemplo** (for example), **tales como** (such as)

### Type 5: Logic and General Knowledge

Use what you know about the world:
> "Después de tres días sin comer, tenía mucha **hambre**."
> (After three days without eating, he was very ___.) → hungry

## 4. Cognates and False Cognates

### Cognates (Cognados)

Words that look similar in Spanish and English and mean the same thing:

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| importante | important |
| información | information |
| problema | problem |
| natural | natural |
| diferente | different |
| familia | family |
| músico | musician |
| teléfono | telephone |
| universidad | university |
| hospital | hospital |

### False Cognates (Falsos amigos)

Words that look similar but have DIFFERENT meanings:

| Spanish | Looks like... | Actually means... |
|---------|--------------|------------------|
| **actual** | actual | current, present-day |
| **embarazada** | embarrassed | pregnant |
| **éxito** | exit | success |
| **librería** | library | bookstore |
| **constipado** | constipated | having a cold |
| **sensible** | sensible | sensitive |
| **realizar** | realize | to carry out, achieve |
| **asistir** | assist | to attend |
| **carpeta** | carpet | folder |
| **fábrica** | fabric | factory |
| **recordar** | record | to remember |
| **soportar** | support | to tolerate, endure |
| **pretender** | pretend | to try to, intend |
| **bizarro** | bizarre | brave, generous |

> Always double-check words that seem "too easy" — they might be false cognates!

## 5. Practice: News Article

Read the following article, then answer the questions.

---

**España apuesta por la energía solar**

*Madrid, 15 de octubre*

El gobierno español ha anunciado un ambicioso plan para incrementar la producción de energía solar en un 40% durante los próximos cinco años. Según el ministro de Transición Ecológica, España tiene una ventaja natural gracias a las más de 2.500 horas de sol al año.

"Nuestro país tiene el potencial de convertirse en el líder europeo de energía renovable", declaró el ministro en una rueda de prensa celebrada en el Ministerio de Transición Ecológica.

El plan incluye la instalación de nuevos parques solares en Andalucía, Extremadura y Castilla-La Mancha, regiones con mayor exposición solar. Además, se ofrecerán incentivos fiscales a las familias que instalen paneles solares en sus viviendas.

Los ecologistas han recibido la noticia con cautela. "Es un paso en la dirección correcta, pero necesitamos más inversión en todas las energías renovables, no solo en la solar", comentó la portavoz de Greenpeace España.

---

### Comprehension Questions

1. What is Spain planning to do?
2. What natural advantage does Spain have?
3. Which regions will get new solar parks?
4. What incentives are offered to families?
5. How did environmentalists react?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Increase solar energy production by 40% in the next five years.
2. More than 2,500 hours of sunshine per year.
3. Andalucía, Extremadura, and Castilla-La Mancha.
4. Tax incentives (incentivos fiscales) for installing solar panels at home.
5. With caution — they think it's a step in the right direction but want more investment in all renewable energy, not just solar.

</details>

### Vocabulary from the Text

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| apostar por | to bet on, commit to |
| incrementar | to increase |
| la ventaja | advantage |
| la rueda de prensa | press conference |
| el parque solar | solar park/farm |
| la exposición solar | sun exposure |
| los incentivos fiscales | tax incentives |
| los paneles solares | solar panels |
| la vivienda | home, dwelling |
| la cautela | caution |
| la inversión | investment |
| el portavoz / la portavoza | spokesperson |

## 6. Practice: Short Story Excerpt

Read the passage, then answer the questions.

---

Era una tarde de noviembre y llovía sin parar. María caminaba por las calles vacías del centro, protegida bajo un pequeño paraguas rojo. No tenía prisa; de hecho, disfrutaba del sonido de la lluvia sobre el asfalto.

De repente, vio algo brillante en el suelo. Se agachó y recogió un reloj antiguo de bolsillo. Era de oro, con las iniciales "J.R." grabadas en la parte trasera. María miró a su alrededor, pero no había nadie. Se guardó el reloj en el bolsillo del abrigo y siguió caminando, preguntándose quién habría perdido algo tan valioso.

---

### Comprehension Questions

1. What was the weather like?
2. What was María carrying?
3. What did she find on the ground?
4. What details did the object have?
5. What did María do with it?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. It was raining continuously (llovía sin parar) on a November afternoon.
2. A small red umbrella (un pequeño paraguas rojo).
3. An old pocket watch (un reloj antiguo de bolsillo).
4. It was gold, with the initials "J.R." engraved on the back.
5. She put it in her coat pocket and kept walking, wondering who had lost something so valuable.

</details>

### Tense Identification

Identify whether each verb is **preterite** (completed action) or **imperfect** (background/description):

1. llovía → ________
2. caminaba → ________
3. vio → ________
4. se agachó → ________
5. era (de oro) → ________

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Imperfect** (scene-setting, weather)
2. **Imperfect** (ongoing action, background)
3. **Preterite** (sudden event that advances the plot)
4. **Preterite** (completed action)
5. **Imperfect** (description of the watch)

</details>

## 9. Annotation Techniques

### Active Reading Marks

Use these marks while reading to engage with the text:

| Mark | Meaning | When to use |
|------|---------|------------|
| ✓ | I understand this | Clear passages |
| ? | I don't understand | Confusing words/sentences |
| ! | Important / surprising | Key information |
| ★ | Key vocabulary | Words to learn |
| → | Connects to something I know | Making connections |

### Steps for Annotating

```
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|          ANNOTATION WORKFLOW                               |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                          |
|  1. First read: DON'T stop. Get the general idea.        |
|                                                          |
|  2. Second read: Mark unknown words with ?                |
|     Try to guess their meaning from context first.        |
|                                                          |
|  3. Third read: Look up remaining ? words.                |
|     Write the meaning in the margin.                     |
|                                                          |
|  4. Summarize: Write 1-2 sentences about the main idea.  |
|                                                          |
|  5. Review: Add ★ words to your vocabulary notebook.      |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
```

## 10. Practice Exercises

### Exercise 1: False Cognate Check

Choose the correct meaning.

1. "El éxito del proyecto fue increíble." — éxito means:
   a) exit  b) success  c) excitement

2. "Asistí a la conferencia ayer." — asistir means:
   a) to assist  b) to attend  c) to insist

3. "Es una persona muy sensible." — sensible means:
   a) sensible  b) sensitive  c) simple

4. "La fábrica produce coches." — fábrica means:
   a) fabric  b) factory  c) fabrication

5. "Estoy constipado." — constipado means:
   a) constipated  b) having a cold  c) constant

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **b) success** 2. **b) to attend** 3. **b) sensitive** 4. **b) factory** 5. **b) having a cold**

</details>

### Exercise 2: Context Clue Practice

Guess the meaning of the underlined word using context clues.

1. "No podíamos ver nada porque había mucha **niebla**; todo estaba blanco y gris."
2. "El agua estaba tan **turbia** que no podíamos ver el fondo del río."
3. "Después del terremoto, muchos edificios quedaron **derrumbados**, completamente destruidos."

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **niebla** = fog (clue: couldn't see anything, everything was white and gray)
2. **turbia** = murky/cloudy (clue: couldn't see the bottom of the river)
3. **derrumbados** = collapsed (clue: synonym provided — "completamente destruidos")

</details>

### Exercise 3: Identify Text Type

What type of text is each excerpt from?

1. "Estimado Sr. López: Le escribo en relación con su pedido número 4521..."
2. "¡2x1 en todos los productos! Solo este fin de semana."
3. "Érase una vez, en un pueblo pequeño, vivía una anciana muy sabia..."
4. "Según el informe del Instituto Nacional de Estadística, el desempleo bajó un 2%..."

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. **Formal email / business letter**
2. **Advertisement / commercial**
3. **Story / fairy tale** ("Érase una vez" = Once upon a time)
4. **News article / report**

</details>
