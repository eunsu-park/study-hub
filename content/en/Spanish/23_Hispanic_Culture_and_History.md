### The Moorish Legacy (711-1492)

For nearly 800 years, the Moors (Muslims from North Africa) ruled much of Spain. Their influence is visible today in:

- **Architecture**: La Alhambra (Granada), Mezquita de Córdoba
- **Language**: ~4,000 Spanish words from Arabic (almohada/pillow, alfombra/carpet, azúcar/sugar, algodón/cotton, ojalá/hopefully)
- **Science**: Mathematics, astronomy, medicine

### The Age of Exploration (1492-)

Christopher Columbus (Cristóbal Colón) reached the Americas in 1492, beginning the Spanish colonization. This resulted in:

- The spread of Spanish language and Catholic religion
- Cultural blending (**mestizaje**) between Indigenous, European, and African peoples
- Exchange of foods: potatoes, tomatoes, chocolate, corn → Europe; horses, wheat, cattle → Americas

### Latin American Independence (1810-1830)

Key figures (**libertadores**):
- **Simón Bolívar** — liberated Venezuela, Colombia, Ecuador, Peru, Bolivia
- **José de San Martín** — liberated Argentina, Chile, Peru
- **Miguel Hidalgo** — initiated Mexican independence (El Grito, September 16, 1810)

## 3. Major Holidays and Celebrations

### Shared Across Many Countries

| Holiday | Date | Description |
|---------|------|-------------|
| Año Nuevo | January 1 | New Year's — Spain: eat 12 grapes at midnight (las doce uvas) |
| Semana Santa | March/April | Holy Week — processions, religious celebrations |
| Día del Trabajo | May 1 | Labor Day |
| Navidad | December 25 | Christmas |
| Nochebuena | December 24 | Christmas Eve — the main family celebration in most countries |
| Día de Reyes | January 6 | Three Kings' Day — gift-giving day in Spain and many L.A. countries |

### Country-Specific Celebrations

| Celebration | Country | Description |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| **Día de los Muertos** | Mexico (Nov 1-2) | Day of the Dead — honoring deceased loved ones with altars (ofrendas), marigold flowers, sugar skulls |
| **San Fermín** | Spain (July 6-14) | Running of the bulls in Pamplona |
| **Las Fallas** | Spain (March 15-19) | Valencia's festival of giant sculptures burned on the final night |
| **La Tomatina** | Spain (August) | Tomato-throwing festival in Buñol, Valencia |
| **Carnaval** | Many countries (Feb/Mar) | Pre-Lenten celebration — especially famous in Barranquilla (Colombia), Cádiz (Spain) |
| **Inti Raymi** | Peru (June 24) | Inca Sun Festival in Cusco |
| **Día de la Independencia** | Mexico (Sept 16) | El Grito — the president reenacts Hidalgo's cry for independence |
| **Quinceañera** | Mexico, C.A. | Girl's 15th birthday celebration marking transition to womanhood |

### Mealtime Culture

```
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|          TYPICAL MEAL SCHEDULE (SPAIN)                     |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                          |
|  Desayuno (7:00-9:00):  Light — coffee, toast, pastry   |
|  Almuerzo (14:00-15:30): Main meal of the day            |
|  Merienda (17:00-18:00): Afternoon snack                 |
|  Cena (21:00-22:30):    Light dinner                     |
|                                                          |
|  NOTE: Latin America generally eats earlier than Spain.   |
|  The main meal is still usually lunch.                    |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
```

> **Sobremesa**: The Spanish tradition of lingering at the table after a meal
> for conversation. This can last an hour or more and is considered an
> important social ritual.

## 8. Social Customs and Etiquette

### Greetings

```
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|          GREETING CUSTOMS                                  |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                          |
|  Spain & Latin America:                                  |
|  • One or two kisses on the cheek between friends         |
|    (Spain: two kisses; many L.A. countries: one kiss)    |
|  • Handshake for formal/business settings                |
|  • Hugs (abrazos) between close friends                  |
|                                                          |
|  Note: Physical greeting customs vary by country          |
|  and have been affected by recent health concerns.       |
+----------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Social Norms

| Concept | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **La familia** | Family is central to Hispanic culture. Extended family (grandparents, cousins, aunts/uncles) often live nearby and gather frequently. |
| **La sobremesa** | Post-meal conversation time; leaving immediately after eating is considered rude. |
| **La siesta** | Afternoon rest tradition (declining in modern cities but still culturally significant). |
| **Punctuality** | Varies by country and context. Social events often start later than stated time. Business meetings are generally punctual. |
| **Tú vs. Usted** | Using the wrong form can be perceived as either too familiar or too cold. When in doubt, use usted. |

### Useful Cultural Vocabulary

| Spanish | English |
|---------|---------|
| la tertulia | regular social gathering for conversation |
| el piropo | street compliment (controversial tradition) |
| el paseo | evening stroll (common in towns/cities) |
| las tapas | small dishes shared socially (Spain) |
| la quinceañera | 15th birthday celebration |
| el compadrazgo | godparent system (social bonds) |

1. What is the sobremesa?
