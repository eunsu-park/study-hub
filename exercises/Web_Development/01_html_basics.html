Exercise 2: Recipe Page

Problem: Create a recipe page with title, ingredient list, cooking instructions, photo, and cooking details.

Classic Margherita Pizza

A simple and delicious Italian classic with fresh ingredients.

Cooking Time
25 minutes (15 min prep + 10 min bake)
Servings
4 people
Difficulty
Easy

Ingredients

  • 1 pizza dough (store-bought or homemade)
  • 200g San Marzano tomatoes, crushed
  • 200g fresh mozzarella, sliced
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)

Cooking Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 250°C (480°F) with a pizza stone or inverted baking sheet inside.
  2. Roll out the pizza dough on a floured surface into a 12-inch circle.
  3. Spread the crushed tomatoes evenly over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border for the crust.
  4. Add the optional minced garlic on top of the sauce.
  5. Distribute the mozzarella slices evenly across the pizza.
  6. Drizzle with 1 tablespoon of olive oil and season with salt and pepper.
  7. Transfer the pizza to the preheated oven and bake for 8–10 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  8. Remove from the oven and top with fresh basil leaves.
  9. Drizzle with the remaining olive oil, slice, and serve immediately.

Understanding Semantic HTML: Why It Matters

Published: November 15, 2024 • 5 min read

Jane Smith
Senior Frontend Developer

Introduction

Semantic HTML is more than just a best practice — it is the foundation of accessible and maintainable web development. In this article, we explore why semantic tags matter and how to use them effectively.

What is Semantic HTML?

Semantic HTML uses elements that clearly describe their meaning to both the browser and the developer. Instead of using generic <div> containers for everything, semantic elements like <article>, <nav>, and <aside> communicate the purpose of each section.

Benefits of Semantic Markup

There are three primary benefits to using semantic HTML:

  1. Accessibility — Screen readers can navigate the page structure more effectively when landmarks are present.
  2. SEO — Search engines better understand content hierarchy and relevance.
  3. Maintainability — Future developers (including yourself) can read and understand the codebase faster.

Conclusion

Start using semantic HTML today. Replace your generic <div> wrappers with meaningful elements and see the difference it makes for accessibility and code clarity.
